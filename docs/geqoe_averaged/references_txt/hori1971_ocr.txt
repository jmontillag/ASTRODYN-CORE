Gen-ichiro HorRI
Department of Astronomy, University of Tokyo, Tokyo
(Received April 10, 1971)
Abstract
The theory of general perturbations originally developed for the solution
of canonical systems
is generalized so as to be applicable to non-canonical
systems.
The features of the original theory are preserved as far as possible.
Perturbed Kepler motion is discussed as an application of the general theory.
Another application to the non-linear oscillations is shown by the solution of
van del Pol’s equation.
Finally the present theory
is compared with the
original theory applied to the generalized canonical system.
Key words:
General perturbations; Non-canonical system; Non-linear os-
cillations.
