Abstract

   Considering a point of polar coordinates (r; m) on an elliptic orbit of semi-major axis a, we set up and compare two algorithms based
                                                                                                                                n
on recurrence relations to compute the Hansen-like coeﬃcients Z n;m   s , which are the coeﬃcients of the expansion of ðr=aÞ exp imm in
Fourier series of the eccentric anomaly. Both Hansen-like coeﬃcients and their derivatives with respect to the eccentricity are considered,
with a special focus on the case 0 6 jmj 6 n arising in the expression of the gravity potential due to a body external to the elliptic orbit.
We provide two eﬃcient algorithms to compute a table of coeﬃcients with a simple recursive process. One algorithm uses some recur-
rence relations linking directly to the Z n;m
                                          s   whereas the other algorithm involves the generalized Laplace coeﬃcients bkp;r (Laskar, 2005).
Numerical behavior of the algorithms is investigated for low and high eccentricities. Both algorithms provide a relative accuracy better
than 1014 for n 6 30. Also, they are at least 10 time faster than an algorithm based on the FFT method (Klioner et al., 1997).
